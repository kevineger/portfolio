Computer Science (CS) is not a required component of most public school’s curriculums. By and large, most students that graduate high-school go without any exposure to CS and all the possible career paths it bears. The large chunk of students who haven’t had the privilege of exploring this field carry a stigma that computer science is making video games or programming in their basement when in reality it is much more.

The purpose of the program is to foster interest in computer science and explore the possibility of persuing it as a career path. The highschool outreach event is covered in depth by UBCO TV in the video embeded on this page and the Irving K. Barber School of Arts and Sciences article.
