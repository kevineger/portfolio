During my fourth year at UBCO, I was asked by the professor of a third year computer science Human Computer Interaction (HCI) class to guest lecture.

I lectured for 80 minutes about color science, typography and frontend web development. My slides are publicly available here. My lecture was focused on providing basic rules and guidelines that students of the class could apply to not only user-focused designed projects but their every day lives. I touched on import color and design choices spanning from their homework assignments to optimizing their IDE.

This was a great experience and I had a lot of fun preparing and delivering the content in a fun and manageable fashion.
